# Principle Guide

What makes a GOOD question for each principle. Every principle must appear in BOTH buy AND pass directions (except circle-of-competence and too-hard which are inherently pass).

## חפיר כלכלי (Moat)
**Target: 12 questions (7 buy / 5 pass)**

BUY examples:
- Company with clear moat + temporary challenge (bad quarter, macro headwind, one-off legal cost)
- Company whose moat isn't obvious (boring business with hidden switching costs)
- Company in competitive market that's quietly building scale advantages

PASS examples:
- Company that HAD a moat but it's eroding (digital disruption, regulation change, new technology)
- Company where everyone thinks there's a moat but switching costs are actually low
- Company with brand recognition but no pricing power (perceived moat ≠ real moat)

Moat types to vary across questions: brand, switching costs, network effects, cost advantages, regulatory/licensing, scale economies, patents (temporary), proprietary data/knowledge.

## רווחי בעלים (Owner Earnings)
**Target: 8 questions (4 buy / 4 pass)**

BUY: Accounting earnings UNDERSTATE true profitability
- High depreciation on fully functional long-life assets (real cash flow >> reported profit)
- Company investing heavily in R&D that's expensed but creates future value
- Working capital improvements releasing cash not visible in income statement

PASS: Accounting earnings OVERSTATE true profitability
- One-time asset sale inflating FCF (not recurring)
- Capitalizing expenses that should be expensed (inflated reported profit)
- FCF looks good because company stopped investing (depleting the business)
- Revenue recognition games — booking multi-year contracts upfront

Key teaching: FCF ≠ accounting profit. Always ask "is this cash REAL and RECURRING?"

## מלכודת ערך (Value Trap)
**Target: 7 questions (2 buy / 5 pass)**

PASS (classic traps):
- Low P/E + high dividend but declining business
- "Cheap on every metric" but the metrics are all backward-looking and deteriorating
- Company where the market is RIGHT that it's cheap for a reason

BUY (contrarian — hardest question type):
- Stock that LOOKS like a value trap but isn't — temporary setback in a structurally strong business
- Hated sector creating opportunity in quality company (e.g., Israeli market post-7/10)
- Company with hidden assets not reflected in market price

Key teaching: Cheap ≠ good value. Sometimes the market is right that something is cheap.

## מרווח ביטחון (Margin of Safety)
**Target: 8 questions (5 buy / 3 pass)**

BUY:
- Trading well below intrinsic value with tangible asset backing
- Post-crisis depression in quality company (market overreacting to temporary event)
- Company with multiple ways to win (even if thesis is partially wrong, you still make money)

PASS:
- Looks cheap on P/E but has no margin of safety due to debt (leverage removes your cushion)
- Cheap valuation but binary outcome (biotech with one drug in trial — no middle ground)
- Company where "cheap" depends on optimistic assumptions about the future

Key teaching: Margin of safety isn't just about P/E being low. It's about: how much can go wrong before I lose money?

## מלכודת צמיחה (Growth Trap)
**Target: 6 questions (1 buy / 5 pass)**

PASS:
- Revenue growing fast but every new customer costs more than they're worth (LTV < CAC)
- Growth funded by dilution (issuing shares to fund losses)
- Growing into a market that's about to commoditize
- "We'll be profitable at scale" with no evidence of improving unit economics

BUY (rare — profitable growth):
- High-growth company with strong unit economics, proven path to profitability
- Company where growth spending is an investment with clear ROI (e.g., opening stores with 2-year payback)

Key teaching: Growth without profitability is value destruction. Revenue ≠ value.

## מעגל כשירות (Circle of Competence)
**Target: 5 questions (0 buy / 5 pass — always pass)**

- Business model too complex or opaque to evaluate with available information
- Company in highly speculative / unpredictable industry (crypto, early-stage biotech)
- Company where the key driver is something you can't assess (regulatory outcome, commodity price)
- Business where the "edge" is claimed but unverifiable

Key teaching: "I don't know" is a valid and profitable investment decision. Not every opportunity is for you.

## סיכון מינוף (Leverage Risk)
**Target: 6 questions (2 buy / 4 pass)**

PASS:
- High debt/equity with declining revenues (can't service debt from shrinking cash flow)
- Company that levered up to do acquisitions that aren't working
- "ROE looks great" but it's entirely driven by leverage — ROIC tells the real story

BUY (manageable leverage):
- Company using moderate debt to fund high-ROIC acquisitions (ROE enhanced by smart leverage)
- Company with fixed-rate long-term debt and growing EBITDA (leverage ratio improving naturally)

Key teaching: ROE can mislead when funded by debt. Always check ROIC alongside ROE. And: debt amplifies both upside AND downside.

## קשה מדי (Too Hard Pile)
**Target: 4 questions (0 buy / 4 pass — always pass)**

- Business that looks interesting but has too many unknowns
- Company where the thesis depends on predicting something unpredictable
- Situation where even the experts disagree wildly on valuation

Key teaching: Buffett's "too hard" pile. You don't have to have an opinion on everything.

## מלכודת מחזוריות (Cyclical Trap)
**Target: 6 questions (3 buy / 3 pass)**

BUY (at cycle bottom):
- High P/E because earnings are at trough — looks "expensive" but actually cheap
- Company with low debt surviving the downturn while competitors fail
- Cyclical company investing in capacity at the bottom (smart management)

PASS (at cycle peak):
- Low P/E because earnings are at peak — looks "cheap" but about to collapse
- Company celebrating "record profits" that are driven by unsustainable commodity prices
- Investors extrapolating peak earnings into the future

Key teaching: In cyclical businesses, buy when P/E is HIGH (trough) and sell when P/E is LOW (peak). This is the OPPOSITE of normal P/E logic.

## שיקום / Turnaround
**Target: 6 questions (3 buy / 3 pass)**

BUY:
- New proven management with skin in the game (buying shares with own money)
- Tangible assets worth more than market cap (real floor value)
- Clear trend improvement (same-store sales turning positive, losses shrinking)
- The three requirements: right people + financial runway + business worth saving

PASS (failed turnaround):
- New management with no track record, just promises
- No tangible asset floor — if turnaround fails, equity is worthless
- Company has been "turning around" for 3+ years with no measurable progress

## קיימות דיבידנד (Dividend Sustainability)
**Target: 5 questions (2 buy / 3 pass)**

BUY:
- Dividend well-covered by FCF (payout ratio 40-60%), growing payout, conservative balance sheet
- Company with contractual/recurring revenue supporting dividend (utilities, infrastructure)

PASS:
- Dividend payout > 100% of FCF (borrowing to pay dividend)
- High yield that's a function of falling stock price, not growing payout
- Company cutting investment to maintain dividend (eating the seed corn)

Key metric: Dividend / FCF ratio. Under 70% = healthy. Over 100% = red flag.

## איכות הנהלה (Management Quality)
**Target: 5 questions (3 buy / 2 pass)**

BUY:
- Management buying shares with own money (skin in the game)
- Proven track record of capital allocation (past acquisitions worked, ROIC improved over time)
- Compensation aligned with shareholders (ROIC-based, not revenue-based)

PASS:
- Insiders selling while promoting the stock publicly
- Excessive compensation relative to company size/performance
- Frequent strategy changes ("pivoting" every year)
- CFO departure (the person who knows the numbers best is leaving)

Key teaching: Watch what management DOES, not what they SAY. Insider buying/selling is the strongest signal available.
